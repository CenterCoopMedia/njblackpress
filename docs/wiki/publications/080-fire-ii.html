Historical notes

No digitized archive was found for Fire II from Trenton, NJ (1975). The title is a direct homage to Fire!! A Quarterly Devoted to the Younger Negro Artists, the landmark Harlem Renaissance literary magazine published in 1926 by Wallace Thurman, Zora Neale Hurston, Aaron Douglas, Langston Hughes, Richard Bruce Nugent, Gwendolyn Bennett, and Countee Cullen. The original Fire!! lasted only one issue due to financial problems and a fire that destroyed remaining copies, but it became one of the most significant publications of the Harlem Renaissance for its unflinching treatment of Black life and its challenge to older Black literary conventions. Hughes wrote that the name was intended 'to burn up a lot of the old, dead conventional Negro-white ideas of the past.' Fire II from Trenton (1975) would have emerged during the Black Arts Movement, which explicitly drew inspiration from the Harlem Renaissance. A reprint of the original Fire!! was issued by Negro University Press in Westport, CT (1970) and by FIRE!! Press in Metuchen, NJ (1982), indicating strong New Jersey connections to preserving this legacy. The modern FIYAH Literary Magazine (speculative fiction) also draws on this lineage. Trenton had one of New Jersey's oldest Black communities, with The Sentinel (est. 1880) being among the first African American newspapers in the state.
