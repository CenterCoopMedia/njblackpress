Historical notes

The North Jersey Connection was a biweekly African American newspaper published in Teaneck, NJ by Connection Publications. The Library of Congress catalogs it with LCCN sn92060412, with the earliest described issue being No. 9 (Oct. 22, 1983). The exact founding date is uncertain but falls in the early 1980s, consistent with the 1982 date. The paper was later succeeded by or renamed to The Connection (Teaneck, N.J.), LCCN sn88071369, which continued publication and grew to describe itself as 'News from a Black perspective' and the 'Largest African-American weekly newspaper in New Jersey.' The Connection was published by Ralph F. Johnson. The newspaper served Bergen County and northern New Jersey's Black community, providing local news coverage from a Black perspective during a period when Teaneck was one of the first communities in the nation to voluntarily desegregate its schools (1965). The Library of Congress holds the catalog record, though physical copies may be held at other libraries. [March 2026 research: LOC latest issue consulted: March 23, 1991 (LCCN sn88071369/sn92060412). Published in Teaneck by Ralph F. Johnson.]
