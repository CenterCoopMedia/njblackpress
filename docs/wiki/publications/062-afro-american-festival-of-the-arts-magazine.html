Mission statement

No formal mission statement located; the issue functioned as a Black Arts forum for showcasing Black writing, performance, and cultural criticism.

Key staff

Editor: Yusef Iman; contributors included LeRoi Jones (Amiri Baraka), Ed Spriggs, Sonia Sanchez, Larry Neal, Joe White, Yusef Inman, and Ben Caldwell.

Historical notes

Published in Newark in August 1966, Afro-American Festival of the Arts Magazine was a small-print Black Arts magazine/anthology issued during LeRoi Jones's first years in Newark, soon after the founding of Spirit House and during the emergence of the Black Arts Movement. George Mason University identifies the volume as edited by Yusef Iman and notes the alternate title 'Anthology of Our Black Selves,' with a 1969 reprint as 'Afro-Arts Anthology: Poetry, Short Stories, Plays.' Brown University's Baraka exhibit identifies the August 1966 issue as containing work by LeRoi Jones, Ed Spriggs, Sonia Sanchez, Larry Neal, Joe White, Yusef Inman, Ben Caldwell, and others. The magazine served Black writers, artists, and Newark-area readers interested in radical Black cultural production, helping document an early network of contributors who would become central to the Black Arts Movement. Only a single 1966 issue is clearly documented under this title, suggesting a short run or one-off publication. [March 2026 research: Single issue, August 1966. Featured Amiri Baraka, Sonia Sanchez, Larry Neal. Held at Brown University.]
