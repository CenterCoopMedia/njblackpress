About this project

Preserving history

A collaborative effort to document and celebrate the legacy of Black journalism in New Jersey.

Our mission

The New Jersey Black Press Database is a research project dedicated to documenting the history and ongoing contributions of Black-owned and Black-focused news publications throughout New Jersey.

From the earliest newspapers of the 1880s to today's digital-first outlets, Black journalists in New Jersey have played a vital role in informing communities, advocating for civil rights, and preserving the stories that mainstream media often overlooked.

This database serves as both an archive and a living resource, connecting researchers, journalists, and community members with the publications that have shaped Black life in the Garden State.
