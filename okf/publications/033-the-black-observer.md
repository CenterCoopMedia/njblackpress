## Historical notes

The Black Observer was a community-oriented newspaper produced by Rutgers-Camden students affiliated with the Black Student Unity Movement (BSUM). A complete surviving issue dates to circa fall 1969. The paper was created by student activists for the broader Camden community during the height of the Black Power movement. The editorial team included Roy Jones, Marie Spearman, Faye Waters, Myrna Williams, Vince Mayfield, Carolyn Williams, Ralph Spencer, Richard Rhodes, Alice Syms, Linda Ellis, R. Eugene Kirkland, Lillian Matthews, Fredia Bodie, and Betty Coleman. A letter from Aaron G. Thompson on behalf of the Rutgers BSUM appears on page 5 of the surviving issue. The complete issue is housed in Special Collections at the Paul Robeson Library, Rutgers University-Camden, as part of the Campus Ephemera Collection within the broader 'Black Student Activism in Camden' collection.
