## Historical notes

No digitized archive was found for En Avant, a Haitian diaspora publication from Newark, NJ (1972). 'En Avant' translates from French as 'Forward' or 'Onward.' Newark's Essex County has one of the largest Haitian communities in New Jersey, concentrated in the Vailsburg section and surrounding cities of Irvington, Orange, and East Orange. The early 1970s saw significant Haitian migration to the Northeast US fleeing the Duvalier dictatorships. Over 72 percent of New Jersey's Haitian population lives within the Archdiocese of Newark. The publication likely served as a community voice for Haitian immigrants during this wave of political exile. Known Haitian diaspora newspapers in the region include Haiti Liberte, which distributes in Newark, and The Haitian Times (founded 1999, Brooklyn). En Avant predates these and may be among the earliest Haitian community publications in New Jersey.
