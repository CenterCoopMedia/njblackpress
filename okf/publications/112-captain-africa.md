## Historical notes

Captain Africa was a Black comic book series created, written, and illustrated by Dwayne J. Ferguson, published by African Prince Productions out of Glen Ridge (and also Somerville), New Jersey. The series debuted with issue #1 in June 1992. The story follows Prince Najee M'Witu, who secretly dons the Falcon Shadow suit of armor (created with his inventor friend Buni Mandella) to protect the high-tech African metropolis of Egyptica from the villain Terror Supreme. Armed with the power of the Black Flame and mastery of the African martial art Kupigani Ngumi, Captain Africa was a pioneering Black superhero of the early 1990s independent comics movement. The series ran three issues (#1 June 1992, #2 October 1993, #3 February 1994) plus an ashcan #0, published through African Prince Productions and Diehard Studios. A graphic novel adaptation, 'Captain Africa: The Battle for Egyptica,' was also published by Africa World Press. Issues are now extremely rare collectors' items. The Internet Archive hosts a borrowable copy of the graphic novel.
