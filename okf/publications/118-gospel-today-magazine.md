## Historical notes

Gospel Today was founded in 1989 by Dr. Teresa Hairston as a four-page newsletter, launched with just $300 while she was a divorced mother of three working two jobs. The newsletter grew into the longest-running and most widely distributed Urban Christian Lifestyle magazine in history. In 1992, Hairston launched companion publication SCOREBOARD (a gospel music industry trade journal), which later became Gospel Industry Today. Content expanded from gospel music coverage to all aspects of Christian lifestyle, featuring interviews with Denzel Washington, Whitney Houston, Evander Holyfield, Jesse Jackson, and others. At its peak, Gospel Today had a circulation of 240,000. In 2008, Lifeway Christian Bookstores pulled the magazine from shelves after it featured women pastors on its cover, conflicting with Southern Baptist beliefs. The magazine never fully recovered. Hairston stepped down in 2011, passing leadership to her son Roland T. Hairston II. The magazine transitioned to digital-only before ceasing operations circa 2013. Hairston also created the Gospel Today TV show (Word TV network) and founded the Gospel Heritage Foundation (1994). Her archival collection, including magazines, audio interviews, 5,000+ sound recordings, and promotional materials, was donated to Indiana University's Archives of African American Music and Culture (AAAMC) in 2016. Hairston published her memoir 'Unstoppable' in 2021. [March 2026 research: Founded 1989 by Dr. Teresa Hairston. Print ceased 2012, brief digital period.
