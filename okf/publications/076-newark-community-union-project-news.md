## Historical notes

The Newark Community Union Project (NCUP) was established in 1964 as part of the Students for a Democratic Society (SDS) Economic Research and Action Project (ERAP), co-led by Tom Hayden and Carl Wittman. NCUP settled into the Clinton Hill neighborhood in South Newark in the summer of 1964, consisting of about 12-15 organizers (half Black, half white college students and graduates). While a specific newsletter titled 'NCUP News' was not confirmed in online sources, NCUP produced community publications and flyers as part of its organizing work in the predominantly Black Clinton Hill neighborhood. The project focused on housing issues, organizing rent strikes (which were illegal in New Jersey), picketing slumlords' homes, and conducting block meetings to address rats, roaches, unsafe living conditions, and police misconduct. NCUP's work is documented in the 1966 documentary film 'Troublemakers.' By late summer 1965, the project staff had dwindled to about half a dozen organizers, though an estimated 150 local people participated in various programs and meetings. The 1967 Newark Rebellion effectively ended NCUP, as the uprising convinced the remaining white organizers they were unwanted. NCUP's methods -- rent strikes, picketing, block meetings -- had by then spread to other parts of the city. Archival materials may exist at the NYU Tamiment Library, Rutgers University Libraries, or the Library of Congress, which catalogs materials under the subject heading 'Newark Community Union Project (N.J.)'.
