## Mission statement

Published weekly 'in interest of Negro race.' Empowered communities of color with a platform to express opinions and interests on local, national, and international current affairs.

## Key staff

Rock, William E.
Howard, Reuben J.

## Historical notes

Founded in 1904 by William Elijah Rock in Long Branch, NJ. Rock was born at Pine Brook, NJ on January 22, 1861, son of Peter and Emma (Schanck) Rock, whose grandfather came from Africa and settled in New Jersey around 1700. Rock was educated in public schools at Pine Brook, received private instruction under Prof. John R. Porter in New York for 4 years, and read law in the office of T.W. Throckmorton in Red Bank for 8 years. The newspaper was named after his Lenni-Lenape mother Emma Schanck-Rock, per her original tribal name translated to English. Publishers listed as Rock and Howard. Relocated from Long Branch to Red Bank in 1909, established with capital of $50. Rock died in June 1933 and the paper folded in 1943. Was described as 'The oldest colored paper in New Jersey.' Per The State Historical Society of Wisconsin's Who's Who of the Colored Race Volume One (1915), Rock is listed alongside Madame C.J. Walker. Original issues available at Red Bank Public Library digital collection.
