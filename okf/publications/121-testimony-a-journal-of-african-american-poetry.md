## Historical notes

Testimony: A Journal of African-American Poetry was a quarterly poetry journal published out of Montclair, NJ, edited by Sandra West. Two issues were published in 1987: Vol. 1, Issue One (Winter 1987) and Vol. 1, Issue Two (Spring 1987). Each issue was in 8.5x11 inch stapled wraps, running 30-34 pages. Issue One contained poetry on Black life, Black uplift, and Black alienation. Issue Two explored the political landscape with a pan-African scope, featuring poems including 'Apartheid,' 'Why Malcolm Had to Leave,' 'Frustrations of a NeoRevolutionary,' and 'Political FatCats.' Notable contributor Sonia Sanchez contributed 'MIA's (Missing in action and other atlantas).' The journal is extremely rare: only four institutional holdings are recorded in OCLC (Rutgers University, New York Public Library, Williams College, and Newark Public Library). No copies are known to be in commerce. The journal represents Montclair's role as a center of Black literary activity in northern New Jersey during the 1980s.
