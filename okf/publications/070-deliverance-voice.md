## Historical notes

No specific archival or historical records were found for the Deliverance Voice in online databases including the Library of Congress, Chronicling America, or the Wikipedia list of African American newspapers in New Jersey. The name suggests a possible connection to a religious organization or church community, as 'Deliverance' is a common term in African American church traditions. The publication appeared in 1967, the same year as the Newark Rebellion (July 12-17, 1967), which was a pivotal event that spurred significant community organizing and media production in Newark's African American community.
