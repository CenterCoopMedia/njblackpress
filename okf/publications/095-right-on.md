## Historical notes

Right On! was the first original fan magazine catering to an African American teen audience, launched in October 1971 by the Laufer Publishing Company. Its first cover stars were The Jackson 5 (Michael, Jermaine, Tito, Jackie, and Marlon Jackson). The magazine was a companion to the white-audience teen magazines Tiger Beat and Bop. Cynthia Horner joined in 1976 as Editorial Director and shaped the magazine's identity for decades, becoming its most notable editor-in-chief. Right On! spawned spinoffs including Word Up! (headquartered in Paramus, NJ, in Bergen County) and Black Beat, all known for centerfold-style pull-out posters. In the 1980s, Right On! became the first publication to cover hip-hop, with stories on The Sugarhill Gang, Grandmaster Flash, and The Furious Five. In the 1990s, the magazine passed through the Sterling/Macfadden publishing conglomerate (formed 1991-1992 when Macfadden's consumer magazines merged with Sterling's Magazines). In 1998, the youth music titles were sold to Primedia; the remaining titles were bought by Dorchester Media in 2004. Right On! ceased regular print publication in 2014. In 2017, Right On! Media Holdings, LLC was formed by Cynthia Horner (CEO), Derrick Horner (Chief Digital Officer), and Kenneth Harris (Chief Marketing Officer) to relaunch as Right On! Digital (rightondigital.com). The Cresskill, NJ listing may reflect a Bergen County office location, as the spinoff Word Up! was based in nearby Paramus, NJ. Some individual issues are available on Internet Archive. [March 2026 research: Print publication ceased circa 2012-2014. Published by Laufer Company. Digital relaunch 2016-2018 by Right On! Media Holdings.].
