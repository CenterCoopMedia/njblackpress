## Historical notes

Utimme Umana/La Voz Oculta was a minority student newspaper at Trenton State College (now The College of New Jersey). The original Utimme Umana was incorporated in 1968 as a Black student newspaper, though it initially struggled to gain footing. On October 2, 1972, the first issue of the combined Utimme Umana: La Voz Oculta was published, merging the Black student paper with a Hispanic voice. 'Utimme Umana' means 'rebirth' in Swahili, while 'La Voz Oculta' means 'the hidden voice' in Spanish. The paper's defining mission was to combat ignorance on the topics of race and it encouraged readers to help spread the word. The publication presented poetry, short stories, and essays relating predominantly to Black, Hispanic, and Latinx themes, along with social and cultural events, announcements, reviews, letters to the editor, photographs, and drawings. It was published twice monthly in newspaper format until early 1978 when it changed to a magazine format, with issues averaging 30 pages. The publication ran from 1972 to 1990. Its founding was part of a broader movement at Trenton State College where, following the assassination of Martin Luther King Jr. in April 1968, African American students presented a list of 10 demands to President Robert Heussler, including creation of an Afro-American Studies program, increased Black student enrollment, hiring Black entertainment, and having a dedicated space on campus for Black students. Digitized issues are available through the TCNJ Digital Repository. [March 2026 research: TCNJ digital archives confirm issues 1972-1990. Student newspaper at Trenton State College (now TCNJ).]
