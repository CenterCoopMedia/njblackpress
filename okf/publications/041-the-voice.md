## Historical notes

The Voice was an African American weekly newspaper published in Plainfield, New Jersey by Voice Associates. It began publication in 1968, notably just one year after the 1967 Plainfield riots (part of the broader Long Hot Summer of 1967), which were a watershed event for the city's African American community. The Library of Congress catalog description is based on Vol. 4, No. 5 (Jan. 24, 1971), confirming it was still publishing at least into its fourth year. The paper was illustrated and measured 44 cm. Its LCCN is sn88063027 and OCLC number is 17430825. The end date of publication is listed as unknown in Library of Congress records. The Library of Congress may not hold physical copies, but other libraries may have holdings.
