## Mission statement

Dramatized the grievances and aspirations of African Americans in New Jersey, serving as a powerful voice for civil rights activities and political organizing in the Black community.

## Historical notes

Published by the Newark Herald Publishing Co. as a weekly African American newspaper. Bibliographic description based on Vol. 11, no. 18 (June 11, 1938). The Herald's founder formed or helped form numerous political organizations including the Negro Progressive Republican League and the Young Men's Colored Republican Association of the City of Newark. Banner headlines addressed issues like 'Poor Housing Conditions are Assailed in Englewood' and 'Negro Must Use Politics to Win Rights.' Succeeded by the New Jersey Herald News (1938-1966). Historian Clement Price noted: 'Had not the black press in New Jersey dramatized the grievances and aspirations of the race, civil rights activities would have been without a powerful voice.' Available on microfilm at Newark Public Library and Rutgers Alexander Library. Newark Public Library has digitized issues in their African American Newark Newspapers collection.
