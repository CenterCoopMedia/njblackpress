## Historical notes

No digitized archive or detailed historical record was found for MEDIC News of Newark, NJ (1971). The publication does not appear in the Newark Public Library microfilm inventory, the Library of Congress directory, or standard African American newspaper bibliographies accessed online. Newark had an active Black health advocacy community during this period; the Concerned Black Nurses of Newark was formed to advocate healthcare for the underserved, and the Newark Public Library curated an exhibit titled 'Dear & Glorious Physician: The History of Black Doctors, Nurses and Hospitals in Newark and Places Just Beyond the River.' The 1970s saw Black communities organizing neighborhood health clinics and demonstration projects to address epidemics of hypertension, diabetes, and infant mortality. MEDIC News may have been connected to one of these community health initiatives.
