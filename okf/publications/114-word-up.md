## Historical notes

Word Up! debuted in August 1987 as a spin-off of Right On! magazine, headquartered in Paramus, NJ under Enoble Media and publisher John Blassingame. Gerrie Summers served as founding editor-in-chief, shortly after replaced by Kate Ferguson. The magazine was a glossy, visually engaging teen publication featuring hip-hop and R&B artist interviews, pin-ups, fashion tips, a pen pals section called 'Pass the Word,' crossword puzzles, and song lyrics. Principal photographer was Ernie Paniccioli. Word Up! was famously immortalized by The Notorious B.I.G. in 'Juicy' (1994): 'It was all a dream; I used to read Word Up! magazine.' Rap Masters was its sister publication, both produced by Faces Magazines in Paramus. The magazine circulated from 1987-2012, proving indispensable in launching careers of countless hip-hop and R&B legends. In the 2020s, Cynthia Horner (former Right On! editor-in-chief) purchased both titles, forming Right On! Media Holdings, LLC. In 2023, the Notorious B.I.G. Estate partnered with Budweiser to release limited-edition tribute issues (5,000 copies each) during Hip-Hop 50 celebrations. [March 2026 research: Last print issue April 2012.
