## Historical notes

Hype Hair was founded in 1992 by editor-in-chief Belinda Trotter, who developed the concept while working as editor-in-chief for the 2 Hype fanzine under the Word Up! publication house in Paramus, NJ. Publisher Scott Figman greenlit the standalone magazine after the hairstyle sections proved enormously popular. Hype Hair became the #1 hair and beauty publication for women of color, published nine times per year featuring hairstyles, beauty products, and celebrity coverage. The magazine was Black women-led since its founding but was only Black-owned from 2013-2017 under UPTOWN Ventures Group. In 2021, Lia Dias (owner of The Girl Cave LA beauty supply stores) purchased the magazine, making it Black-owned again. Dias merged her beauty supply chain with the brand to launch Hype Hair Beauty Supply stores in California and Texas. The magazine is now digital-only, headquartered in Los Angeles.
