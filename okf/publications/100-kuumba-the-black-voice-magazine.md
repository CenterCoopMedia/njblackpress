## Historical notes

No digitized archive was found for Kuumba: The Black Voice Magazine from New Brunswick, NJ (1974-1975). 'Kuumba' is a Swahili word meaning 'creativity,' one of the seven principles of Kwanzaa (Nguzo Saba). The name was widely adopted by Black student organizations and cultural groups during the 1970s Black Arts and Black Power movements. During this period, Rutgers University-New Brunswick had an active Black student community; the Black Voice Carta Latina (BVCL) was a later Black/Hispanic student publication at Rutgers, suggesting a lineage of Black student media on campus. The Scarlet & Black Research Center at Rutgers documents Black voices at the university and may have records. Rutgers University Special Collections and University Archives would be the most likely repository for this student publication. Livingston College, established in 1969 as Rutgers' most diverse residential college, was a hub for Black student activism and publications during this era.
