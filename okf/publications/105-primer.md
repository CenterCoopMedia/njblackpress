## Historical notes

No digitized archive or detailed historical record was found for Primer from East Orange, NJ (1973-1978). The publication does not appear in the Library of Congress newspaper directory, the Wikipedia list of African American newspapers in New Jersey, or the Newark Public Library microfilm inventory. East Orange, in Essex County, had a growing African American population during the 1970s and was served by several Black publications. The Essex Forum (est. 1972) was a biweekly Black newspaper serving Orange, Montclair, and East Orange during the same period. The broader Essex County area was also served by the New Jersey Afro American (Newark, 1941-1991) and the Baraka-era publications (Black NewArk, Unity and Struggle). The East Orange Public Library and the Newark Public Library's New Jersey Information Center (973-733-7775, njreference@npl.org) may hold physical copies. The reference work 'African-American Newspapers and Periodicals: A National Bibliography' (Danky & Hady, Harvard University Press, 1998) may also list this title.
