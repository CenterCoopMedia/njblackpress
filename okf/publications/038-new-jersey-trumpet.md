## Mission statement

Served the Black community in Jersey City and Newark during the late 19th century as a voice for African American interests in New Jersey.

## Key staff

Publisher: William Murrell.

## Historical notes

A weekly newspaper published by Col. William H. Murrell (listed as 'Wm. Murrell'). First issue December 29, 1887; ceased around 1897. Originally based in Jersey City, it relocated to Newark in 1889. Murrell was born into slavery in Georgia and served during the Civil War as a valet to Confederate General Longstreet, later enlisting in the 44th regiment Virginia and South Carolina troops. After the war he moved to Louisiana, served in the State Legislature for nine years, and was a colonel of the 4th Regiment, Louisiana State National Guard. After losing his possessions in the great flood of 1878 and receiving death threats from the KKK, he moved to Washington D.C. (Treasury Department), then to New Jersey in 1883 where he established The Trumpet. His wife Louisiana served as business manager. Two of their sons, William Frederick and Wellington, were killed in the Spanish-American War and are buried in Arlington National Cemetery.
