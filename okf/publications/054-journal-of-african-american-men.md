## Historical notes

The Journal of African American Men was an academic quarterly published by Transaction Publishers in New Brunswick, NJ, from Vol. 1, No. 1 (Summer 1995) through Vol. 6, No. 4 (Spring 2002). It served as the official publication of the National Council of African American Men. The journal covered a range of scholarly topics relevant to African American men, including a notable special issue on 'Impacts of Incarceration on the African American Family' (Summer 2001). Transaction Publishers, based at Rutgers University in New Brunswick, was a leading academic publisher of social science journals and books. The Journal of African American Men was succeeded by the Journal of African American Studies, which continues publication. The complete archive is available through JSTOR.
