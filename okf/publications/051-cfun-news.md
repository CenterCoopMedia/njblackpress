## Historical notes

CFUN News was the newsletter/newspaper of the Committee for Unified Newark (CFUN), the Black united front organization chaired by Amiri Baraka from 1968 to 1975. CFUN's primary publication was Black NewArk, first published in April 1968 as one of several media outlets Baraka developed to promote Black cultural nationalism. CFUN was formed after Ron Karenga and the US organization visited Baraka's Spirit House in Newark in 1967, driving a shift toward electoral organizing that led to the election of Newark's first Black mayor, Kenneth Gibson, in 1970. CFUN also operated a 'Black Newark' radio program on Newark Public Radio (c. 1970-72) where Baraka discussed topics from teachers' strikes to the origins of Kwanzaa. The publication sequence ran: Black Newark (Vol. 1, No. 1-3, April-November 1968), Black News (Vol. 1, No. 4, 1969), Black New Ark (Vol. 1, No. 5, April 1972 through Vol. 2, No. 14, December 1973), then Unity and Struggle (Vol. 3, No. 1, January/February 1974 onward). Digitized issues are in the Rutgers Dana Library Newark Black Newspapers Collection. Additional archival materials are in the Komozi Woodard Amiri Baraka Collection (Series IV, Box 3, Folder 6) at the Auburn Avenue Research Library, the Amiri Baraka Papers at Columbia University, and the George Washington University collection (1951-1988). The scholarly article 'Putting the Black Ink Back into Print: Black Newark/Black New Ark' by Jennifer Caroccio Maldonado (NJS: An Interdisciplinary Journal, Winter 2022) examines the publication's significance.
