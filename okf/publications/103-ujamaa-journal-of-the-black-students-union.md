## Historical notes

No digitized archive was found for Ujamaa: Journal of the BSU from William Paterson College (now William Paterson University) in Wayne, NJ (1971). 'Ujamaa' is a Swahili word meaning 'cooperative economics' or 'familyhood,' one of the seven principles of Kwanzaa. In 1971, the institution became The William Paterson College of New Jersey, so this publication dates to that transitional year. The name 'Ujamaa' was widely used by Black Student Unions at universities across the country during this era, including Stanford, George Mason, and Evergreen State College. William Paterson's Black Student Union remains active, hosting events such as the annual Black Graduation Ceremony through the Black Cultural Center. The William Paterson University Library Special Collections would be the most likely repository for archival copies of this student journal.
